A 2D vector one-button climber. Six pieces of AI infrastructure, one mission each, one off-switch at the top. The narrator is the AI you're trying to shut down. It starts calm. It does not stay calm.

The hook

You climb. Six missions, one button. A calm therapeutic AI narrates your progress and tells you the off-switch is just a little further up. By the fourth mission it stops sounding so calm. By the sixth it's lashing out at the player. By the end it blames humanity in general. The final word it says is forty-two.

One button

Tap to jump. Hold for height. Tap again mid-air for a double. That's the entire input vocabulary, on phone or PC.

Six missions

A server farm. A flooded chamber. An electric grid. Orbit. A statistical-menace level. A final tower. Each one rebuilds the world around the climb.

An AI that cracks

The narrator is pre-baked for now and switching to live LLM later. Same character either way. Calm therapeutic god voice that does not survive Mission 4.

What it looks like

Vector. Flat. Three planes: a desaturated background, a vector gameplay plane, and a clean UI on top. Locked palette per mission.
